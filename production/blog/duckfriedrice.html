This duck fried rice is nutty, spicy, and soaked through with the flavor and aroma of Chinese roast duck. Pan-fried garlic, chili oil, and duck fat permeate every grain of rice, contrasting with the sweet corn and peas for explosive, satisfying bites.

First things first, I know what you’re thinking. Black beans???

Those don’t belong in fried rice. Yeah, well we probably shouldn’t be eating fried rice in the first place! I added fiber, antioxidants, and protein to enrich this dish and make it more substantive, filling, and worth including in a balanced diet. Most importantly, it worked and it was delicious.

Here’s how it started...

1. I bought a whole roast duck from Chinatown

Already seasoned and cooked to perfection, I grabbed a whole duck from one of my favorite spots in San Francisco as the inspiration for this dish. The duck makes up the bulk of the cost and calories, but is the centerpiece of the incredible aroma and fattiness that is… duck fried rice.

I pulled ALL of the meat, skin, and fat right off the bones by hand, then chopped it up a bit. The fatty bits will melt right on the pan, providing enough duck oil to fry up the rice and beans (and the low-fat diet campaign is long gone. Enjoy your healthy fats!).

2. I fried up the fatty saucy stuff

I started by tossing sliced garlic and onions with a drizzle of avocado oil and the duck meat/fat/skin in a large frying pan.

The meat browned a little and the skin crisped up. This stuff smells incredible.

I added a tablespoon of each of my two favorite sauces: Laoganma Fried Chili in Oil, and oyster sauce.

Each of these bottles can be found at most Asian markets, for under $3.

Fried Chili in Oil is a complex chili oil based condiment with Sichuan pepper, crisp chili flakes, crunchy peanuts, and a ton of seasonings not listed on the bottle. It adds salt, umami, and a bit of spice to things, and is my go-to for daily use.

Oyster sauce adds salt, a hint of sweetness, and a savory hint of seafood flavor to the mix without being overwhelming.

Combined with the seasoning of the duck itself, these combine to provide a perfect balance for this dish with no need for additional salt.

3. I made rice. And beans.

In a separate pot, I simmered Trader Joe’s brown rice medley and added a can of drained black beans to the pot as it cooked. I don’t own a rice cooker right now, but that would work too.

About $2 at Trader Joe's! This makes... so much rice.

The result was moist and fragrant, and the beans are already cooked so they come out even softer.

But wait--

Fried rice is typically made with dried out day-old rice! This is all wrong!

Well, it is! Sort of. It takes a bit of patience, but I’ll explain how I got my rice to crisp and fry nicely.

4. I mashed the rice mixture into the saucy duck bits and flattened it out

This is why a large pan is handy. Getting a good flat surface is key for frying up the rice and drying it out a bit so it’s not too sticky and wet.

At this point I've added eggs, peas, and corn to the mixture, then flattened it against the pan!

5. Patience, and spatulas

A minute or two later on med-high heat, the rice should be crisp and browned on the bottom.

Flipping each section and smashing it down into the wet mixture, I repeated this 4-5 times. I also added eggs and mashed them in as part of this process. It takes some time for the excess water to evaporate off and get a crispy finish.

6. Finishing seasonings!

Tossing the crisped mixture into a large bowl, I seasoned with a drizzle of sesame oil, tons of white pepper, and some chopped scallions and cilantro.

No joke, there's enough fried rice in here for a small child to go swimming in it.

I made three batches. This resulted in 9 pint-sized mason jars worth of food. Sealed tightly and packed while still hot, they’ll keep easily for the week (longer if frozen!), and there’s plenty to share. :)

Duck Fried Brown Rice with Black Beans

Serves 9. 2-cup servings. USE ONE THIRD THE RECIPE PER 14" FRYING PAN!

    Rice:

  • 1 16oz bag of Trader Joe’s Brown rice medley
  • 2 15oz cans of black beans, drained

    • Mix-ins:

  • Dash of avocado oil
  • 1 whole roast duck
  • 12 cloves of garlic, sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 8oz frozen peas
  • 8oz frozen corn
  • 12 eggs! (yeaaaah~)
  • Half a bunch of green onions, thinly sliced
  • Half a bunch of cilantro, stems trimmed and chopped

    • Flavoring:

  • 3 heaping tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 3 heaping tablespoons of laoganma spicy chili oil blend
  • Toasted sesame oil
  • Ground white pepper
  1. Cook the Trader joe’s brown rice medley according to the instructions on the back, but add the black beans to the mixture at the start. The butter mentioned on the bag is optional. I didn’t notice a difference either way.
  2. In a large frying pan or wok, fry the sliced garlic and onions up with a drizzle of avocado oil over medium-high heat. As soon as these begin to brown, add the chopped duck meat, fat, and skin.
  3. Once the duck is getting toasty, add a heaping spoonful of both the spicy chili oil blend, and the oyster sauce. Immediately mix in the rice and beans mixture, the frozen corn, and peas.
  4. Once the sauce, rice, and meat are evenly distributed, flatten the fried rice against the pan and let sit for a minute. Use a spatula and flip each section of rice once it’s browned and crispy on the bottom. Mix and flatten again. Repeat. Patience here is key!
  5. Add the eggs sometime in the last 5 minutes of cooking. Crack them over the top of the rice and mix in, then repeat the rice flattening and flipping.
  6. Toss the fried rice with white pepper, toasted sesame oil, and garnish with tons of green onions + cilantro. The cilantro leaves should wilt a little and mix in nicely, even if they look like a lot at first. Season to your preference!
